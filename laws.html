WILDLIFE WARRIORS

Laws that Uphold Animal Rights

Everyone holds a duty of being compassionate and empathize with all living creatures.

To kill any animal including stray animals is a punishable offence.

You can land in a prison for up to 3 months if a pet is abandoned regardless of the reason.

No animal, including chickens, can be slaughtered in any place other than a slaughterhouse.

Sick or pregnant animals shall not be slaughtered.

Stray dogs that have been operated for birth control cannot be captured by anybody including any authority.

Not providing a pet with the elements important for survival including access to food, water, shelter or keeping it chained for hours can lead to imprisonment.

Monkeys cannot be owned.

Bears, monkeys, tigers and lions are prohibited from being trained and used for entertainment purposes.

Animal sacrifice is illegal.

Holding or organising an animal fight is a cognizable offence.

Experimentation and cosmetics test on animals is banned.

Teasing or disturbing the animals in a zoo and littering the zoo premises is an offence punishable by a fine or imprisonment.

Trapping, poisoning or baiting of any animal leads to a fine or an imprisonment.

Destroying the eggs or the nests of birds and reptiles or chopping a tree with the nests of birds and reptiles has a fine and imprisonment.

Carrying animals (in a vehicle) where they are in discomfort or in a position causing pain or suffering is a punishable offence.
